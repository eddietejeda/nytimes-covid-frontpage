About

I wanted to track coverage of COVID-19 over time and pinpoint precisely when the virus stopped being frontpage news. More specifically, I want to know when we would have three consecutive days where COVID-19 was not frontpage news. This site uses a script that downloads a PDF of the New York Times front page and looks for the following keywords: corona, covid, virus, pandemic, wuhan.

Conclusion

This project is complete. According to this data, COVID-19-related news started appearing on the frontpage on January 21, 2020.

It took 353 days after the first mention for there to be no mention of COVID on the front page. But the streak did not last for more than two days. The headlines that took precedence were:

  • January 7th 2021 - Trump Incites Mob
  • January 8th 2021 - Calls Grow To Remove Trump As U.S. Officials Head for Exit

After 834 days, we had 3 consecutive days. The headlines that took precedence were:

  • May 3, 2022 - Leaked Draft Shows Court Would Topple Roe v. Wade
  • May 4, 2022 - Draft Abortion Ruling Signals Seismic Political Shift
  • May 5, 2022 - Leak Intensifies View That Court Is Too Political
